The Roadmap

Gateway done right is a six-month to two-year arc, not a thirty-day challenge. Here is the honest pace, the self-diagnosis framework, and the question you answer for yourself.

How to use this

  • When: Each evening, as you unwind. 45–60 min block. Headphones required.
  • Where: Bed, couch, or floor. Supine is traditional. A chair is fine.
  • Journal: One notebook. Date + tape + three lines minimum. The journaling IS the training.
  • Repetition > progression. A tape repeated four nights beats four tapes rushed through.
  • Miss a day: Don't double up. Resume where you left off.

Season 1 (Months 1–2) — Wave I: Ground the Body

Nightly Wave I on rotation. Build Focus 10 — mind awake, body asleep. By end of Month 2 you should have 30+ sessions logged and REBAL reliable.

Test: Can you hit F10 with only breath, no tape? If yes → Wave II. If no → repeat Wave I. No shame in another month on the foundation.

Season 2 (Months 3–4) — Wave II: Expand

Wave II replaces most sessions. Keep 1–2 Wave I tapes per week as maintenance. Learn Problem-Solving, Patterning, and the Energy Bar Tool.

Test: Has Problem-Solving produced a workable answer? Has a Patterning outcome manifested, even partially? If yes → Wave III. If no → stay in II, tighten question quality.

Season 3 (Months 5–6) — Wave III: Integrate

Daylight integration phase. Tapes 4x/week. The Codes become automatic in your day — REBAL entering a room, EBT before a pitch, the box for runaway worry.

Test: Are you measurably healthier than six months ago? Sleep, HRV, mood, decision quality, relationships. If not — stop climbing. Fix the foundation first.

Season 4 (Months 7–8) — Wave IV: No Time

F15 work. Longer and heavier sessions; reduce tape frequency to 3–4x/week. NVC, compoint, one-year patterning.

Test: Have you had an experience you genuinely can't explain? Write it down verbatim. This is your first empirical hit.

Season 5 (Months 9–10) — Wave V: The Bridge

F21 work. The most demanding. Do not rush here if Wave IV isn't landing. Your journal at end of Month 10 is your lab notebook.

Test: After 30 F21 sessions, what's your honest verdict? The data IS the experience.

Season 6 (Months 11–12) — Wave VI: Ongoing Practice

Odyssey tracks + self-directed F21. You're no longer running Gateway. Gateway is now part of your OS.

Self-Diagnosis: Baseline (do this tonight)

Score each 1–10. Write today's date in the journal. Re-score every 30 days.

  • Sleep quality (avg past 7 nights)
  • Focus — sustain attention 45 min without phone
  • Emotional regulation — recovery from spikes
  • Physical energy (3pm)
  • Pain frequency (times/week)
  • Decision clarity
  • Creative flow
  • Relationship quality (3 closest)
  • Body awareness
  • Intuitive hit rate

Green flags — something real is happening

  • Sleep objectively improves (Oura, Apple Watch, journal — pick a metric)
  • Decisions get cleaner
  • Physical symptoms shift
  • Dream recall sharpens without trying
  • Intuitive hits stack up — you call things before they happen
  • People notice, unprompted
  • One "that shouldn't have happened" event. Write it down verbatim.

Red flags — you're not doing it right, or this isn't for you

  • 3+ weeks in and falling asleep every session → go to bed earlier before the tape
  • Zero hits in 60 days → check headphones, stereo separation, focus quality
  • Getting worse (anxiety, dissociation) → stop. Back off to Release & Recharge only for 2 weeks
  • Reporting things you didn't actually feel → ego hijacking. Return to honest journaling

The placebo question

At Wave I–III: You can't fully rule placebo out — and it doesn't matter. Placebo that heals is still healing. Sleep better, think clearer, regulate emotion — the mechanism is secondary.

At Wave IV–V: The question sharpens. If you're receiving accurate information you didn't have before (predictions that land, remote viewing hits), placebo doesn't explain it. Collect specific, dated, checkable predictions. See what cashes out.

The deal with yourself

You're running a 12-month experiment on your own OS. Scientist and subject. Tapes are the intervention. Journal is the data. Body is the lab.

At the end of 12 months, one of three things is true:

  1. It was real and it rewired you. Gains are measurable. Codes are installed. Welcome to the other side of the door.
  2. Placebo — but the placebo healed you. Sleep, focus, mood all better. You don't care what the mechanism was. You win.
  3. Nothing happened. 60+ sessions, honest work, no change. Also a valid result. You cross it off — but you actually tried, not just dismissed it.

All three are fine. The only bad outcome is the half-committed version that leaves you with a vague "I tried that once."

Show up. Measure. Decide.

The data doesn't care whether you're a believer or a skeptic. Only whether you did the work.
